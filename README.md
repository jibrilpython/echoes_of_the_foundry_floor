# Echoes of the Foundry floor

Description:

Echoes of the Foundry floor is a mobile application for collectors of vintage pattern-making antiquities, historians of industrial casting, and keepers of heavy metallurgical heritage, dedicated to cataloging the wooden sand-casting molds, custom shrinkage rulers, and manual rammers used to shape the molten iron skeletons of early machinery before the era of modern 3D sand-printing and automated die-casting.
Systematize your collection of the masters of the mold—from intricate segmented wooden gear patterns meticulously carved to account for metal cooling contraction to heavy cast-iron core boxes, from specialized foundry molding trowels to manual pneumatic sand rammers that packed the casting beds.
Create a digital archive of heavy industry by recording the instrument type, manufacturer, temperature range, era, and the specific steel mill, foundry, or ceramic kiln each specimen once calibrated.

## Bespoke Object Fields (Model "Foundry Artifact"):

•	Mold Matrix Code: A unique, system-generated foundry production registration hash (e.g., EFF-MOLD-8831-IRON-P).

•	Pattern Classification: The category of sand-casting component (e.g., Split Pattern, Core Box, Match-Plate, Molding Slick, Shrinkage Rule).

•	Artisan Hallmark: Fictionalized machine pattern works and industrial drafting firms (e.g., Sovereign Pattern Works, IronShore Die & Template, Vanguard Casting Supply).

•	Shrinkage Allowance Factor: The specific fractional mathematical scale built into the tool to compensate for cooling contraction (e.g., 1/8-inch per foot for cast iron, 3/16-inch per foot for brass).

•	Draft Angle Geometry: The specific degree of taper carved into the vertical walls to allow clean extraction from the casting sand (e.g., 2-degree positive taper, deep bevel profile).

•	Material & Joinery Seal: The exact composition of the pattern or tool (e.g., Shellac-finished clear pine, laminated mahogany, cast zinc inserts, oil-rubbed bronze).
•	Volumetric Footprint: The absolute spatial displacement volume of the mold pocket and external dimensions in centimeters.

•	Preservation Soundness: The physical state of the casting interface (e.g., Wood warping tolerance, sand abrasion scoring, shellac flaking percentage).

•	Smelting Ground Zero: The fictionalized manufacturing city, heavy locomotive plant, or naval shipyard forge floor where the pattern was poured (e.g., Great Lakes locomotive engine works, historic mid-atlantic pump foundry, forgotten maritime engine shop).
